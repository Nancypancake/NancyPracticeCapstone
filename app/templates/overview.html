Overview

Background

Roughly 85% of people worldwide have low self-esteem. There are a plethora of root causes: fear of failure, difficult/unhappy childhood, poor academic performance, abusive relationships, social media, etc.

Dangers of Low Self-Esteem

Having a smaller feeling of self-worth can affect individuals physically, emotionally, and mentally. It can cause a large range of anxiety, from worried thoughts to physical problems like high blood pressure. It can lead to depression - depressed mood or loss of pleasure or interest in activities for long periods of time. Lastly, it can lead to self-harm.
